I am an Assistant Professor in the East Asian College at Yonsei University and an international security scholar specializing in empirical research on interstate conflict and diplomacy. Driven by this research agenda, I apply experimental and computational social science methods to examine the dynamics of conflict escalation and de-escalation, particularly within the context of U.S.-China great power competition.

My research has been published in International Studies Quarterly, Journal of Conflict Resolution, The Journal of Experimental Political Science, Political Science Research and Methods, and Research & Politics. These projects have garnered substantial support, amounting to over 115,000 USD.

I received my Ph.D. in Political Science from Florida State University in 2024. During the 2023-2024 academic year, I was the Lo Chia-Luen International Sinology Visiting Fellow at National Chengchi University.
